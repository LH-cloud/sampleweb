This new McDonald's restaurant looks like a Qatari tent!

The newest McDonald's restaurant at Al Bayt Stadium is the current talk of the town!
If you haven't seen it yet, it's definitely something worth checking out. McDonald's Qatar marks a milestone with the opening of this flagship restaurant with a unique style, which is also its 50th branch in the country. It's strategically located on the grounds of a first-class venue for the FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022â„¢ along with a beautiful greenery scene. The restaurant mimics the design of Al Bayt Stadium, which is inspired by tents of nomadic tribes and families in Qatar and the Gulf region. Black stripes can identify the bayt al sha'ar on the exterior and vibrant sadu patterns inside. The state-of-the-art branch is now open to everyone and fully functioning 24/7 so you can visit anytime. According to McDonald's Qatar, the interiors of the Al Bayt branch is different from the look of its other branches in Qatar, elevating the interior concept for QSRs in the country. They have also introduced their first digital Drive-Thru in Qatar on this branch, giving a new look and feel to the usual McDonald's drive-thru.
There's also a McCafe where you can enjoy a selection of beverages and pastries as well as a play area for families that will be opened soon. The fully-equipped restaurant offers Breakfast, Delivery, and an outside seating area.
McDonald's Qatar follows a set of enhanced hygiene and safety measures as stipulated by the Ministry of Public Health and the Ministry of Commerce and Industry to prevent the spread of COVID-19. This includes temperature checks, Ehteraz app green status, provision of alcohol-based sanitizer, contact-free ordering, contactless payment, and ensuring physical distancing for customers.

The United States and Iran have been stuck in a stupid game of chicken. Leaders of both countries say they want to restore the Iran nuclear deal to its original state: with the U.S. as a member and Iran in full compliance with its strictures.
